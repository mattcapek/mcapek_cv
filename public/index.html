curriculum vitae
I’m driven by a fascination with how animal behavior evolves—from molecules to neural circuits. I’m especially interested in how sensory systems drive adaptation and evolution, and how interactions between species give rise to novel behaviors.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow in the lab of Dr. Nicholas Bellono at Harvard University, where I study how cross-kingdom communication drives evolution and speciation, and how these processes shaped Earth’s earliest terrestrial ecosystems.

I completed my PhD in the lab of Dr. Marco Gallio at Northwestern University, where I studied the evolution of temperature sensing and preference behavior in Drosophila, combining molecular, genetic, and behavioral approaches to understand how animals adapt to different thermal environments.
